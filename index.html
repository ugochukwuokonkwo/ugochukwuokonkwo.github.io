Low-Cost Versatile Electrospinning System

Undergraduate Senior Design Project focused on the design, fabrication, integration, and validation of a low-cost electrospinning platform capable of producing both aligned and random polymeric nanofibers for tissue engineering applications.

  • Developed the overall system architecture and integrated the syringe pump, high-voltage power supply, rotating collector, enclosure, and control systems into a unified electrospinning platform.
  • Designed the complete enclosure and support structure using CAD software, creating a modular aluminum-frame and acrylic-panel system for safe operation and maintenance accessibility.
  • Fabricated and assembled the enclosure using aluminum extrusions, acrylic panels, fastening systems, tapping operations, and machining processes.
  • Integrated mechanical, electrical, and motion-control subsystems including the syringe pump, NEMA17 stepper motor, rotating drum collector, and associated control electronics.
  • Applied iterative prototyping and additive manufacturing methods to develop custom mounts, supports, and motion-control components.
  • Conducted testing and parameter optimization involving flow rate, collector speed, and polymer delivery conditions to improve nanofiber consistency and alignment.
  • Implemented safety-focused design features including enclosure protection, grounding considerations, and high-voltage isolation measures.

Custom 3D Printed Components

  • Rotating Drum Collector: Designed a custom collector assembly capable of producing aligned nanofibers through controlled rotational motion.
  • Collector Support Structure: Developed custom mounting and support components to maintain collector alignment and structural stability.
  • Motor Mount Assembly: Designed a dedicated motor mounting system to couple the collector drive motor with the rotating drum while maintaining alignment and minimizing vibration.
  • Syringe Pump Base: Created a custom mounting platform to securely support the syringe delivery system and improve assembly accessibility.
  • Stepper Motor Holder: Designed a custom NEMA17 motor mount to support accurate and repeatable polymer delivery.
  • Syringe Pusher Mechanism: Developed a custom linear actuation component to translate motor motion into controlled syringe plunger displacement for consistent flow-rate control.
